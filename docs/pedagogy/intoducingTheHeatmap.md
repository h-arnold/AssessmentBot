Fundamental to Assessment Bot's functionality is the heatmap - a visual method of representing a class or cohorts' performance on a given task. The premise behind it is simple: you are looking for clusters of 'heat' (red and orange) to identify areas of weakness that need to be addressed.

In Assessment Bot, each task is rated from 1-5 for Completeness, Accuracy ans SPaG (spelling, punctuation and grammar). To arrive at a score, it compares a student's work on a given task with the 'reference task' that you supplied earlier. The reference task should score what you would award as '5' for all criteria. 1, therefore is highlighted in red, 3 amber and green for 5. You also have grey, which is accompanied by an 'N' for when a student hasn't attempted the work and a white 'E' when there's been an error in extracting the data for that task.

Most of the time therefore, you will be looking for clusters of red and amber where the work has been attempted but is either incomplete or inaccurate, and grey, where the work has not been attempted before.

### Clusters from specific groups

Here, I noticed that students [a b and c] are way off track from where they need to be in this task. Taking a look at their work, I can see that these students have all misunderstood [x y and z] allowing me to address this with these students as a group in their next lesson.

## Key Points

You'll notice that in all of the examples above, I used the heatmap to help me decide **where to look**. This tool is not designed to do the formative assessment for you, but to help you focus your attention in the right area so you can act upon the insights it provides. By doing this, you remain in full control of the assessment process, but hopefully feeling a little less overwhelmed in doing so!
