# Making use of the overview sheets

As a head of department, being able to collate and review the data from entire cohorts is incredibly useful in tracking progress at a macro level. This feature is simple and crude at the moment but I've got grand plans to improve on it. If you're interested, check out the [issues on Github](changeThisFromAPlaceholderPlz) for ideas on how you can contribute to make it even better.

To help you make the most of it, here's how I use the overview sheets to help me track progress.

## Identifying students who have 'slipped through the cracks'

When you combine having many classes, large class sizes and some students who excel at looking busy, you inevitably end up with students who slip through the cracks, and it may take 2-3 weeks before you identify that a student has done very little. On the flip side, sometimes you find students who are discretely brilliant and having this show up on the heatmap can be incredibly valuable so that you know to celebrate their success.

Where this becomes particularly powerful when done at a whole cohort level, is that it enables you to identify exactly which students these are, which classes they're in and in what topics they are struggling. In most of my department meetings, I set aside a little time to look at the top 20 lowest performing students and we discuss why. 

Tip: Make sure you do this transparently and non-judgementally - otherwise you're just encouragaing your colleagues to get students to do work that looks nice, but isn't a reflection of their ability. I always start by exploring the reasons the my students on that list are underperforming.
